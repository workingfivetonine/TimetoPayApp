Scanning Receipts

Scanning a receipt is the fastest way to track your grocery spending. The AI reads every item and price so you don't have to type anything.

Scanning an Image

1
Tap ScanClick the Scan button in the bottom navigation bar.
2
Upload a photoSelect a receipt photo from your device. JPG and PNG work best.
3
Wait for the AIThe receipt is read automatically — this usually takes 5–15 seconds.
4
Review and saveCheck the extracted items and prices, make any corrections, then tap Save.
Tips for better scans: Use good lighting, lay the receipt flat, make sure the whole receipt is visible, and avoid shadows. Crumpled or faded receipts may produce less accurate results.

Scanning a PDF

If your store emails you digital receipts as PDFs, you can upload them directly:

  1. Tap Scan → Upload a PDF
  2. Select your PDF receipt (up to 4 pages)
  3. The AI processes each page — longer receipts may take 15–30 seconds
  4. Review the results and save

Reviewing Extracted Items

After scanning you'll see a review screen showing the store name, purchase date, each item with its price, and the receipt total. You can edit any field before saving. If an item has a generic name like "ITEM 1234", tap it to rename it something useful like "Whole milk."

Managing Stores

Every receipt is linked to a store, and prices are tracked and compared across stores over time. The more stores you add, the more useful your price history becomes.

Adding a Store

1
Go to StoresTap Stores in the navigation menu.
2
Tap Add StoreEnter the store name — e.g. "Costco", "Trader Joe's", "Stop & Shop".
3
Add optional detailsAddress, phone, hours, and delivery information are optional but helpful.
4
SaveThe store is now available when you scan receipts.

Editing or Deleting a Store

Tap any store in your list to open it, then tap Edit to update its details or scroll to the bottom to delete it. Deleting a store also removes all receipts associated with it.

Shopping List

Your shopping list builds itself automatically from your receipt history. Items you buy regularly appear on the list so you don't have to add them manually.

How Items Appear

An item shows on your list when you've purchased it before or manually added it, and haven't dismissed it. Items are automatically sorted by store so you can shop efficiently.

Adding Items Manually

  1. Go to Shopping List
  2. Tap Add item or browse the Catalog
  3. Select items to add them to your list

Using Your List While Shopping

  • Check off items as you put them in your cart
  • See the best price from your history for each item
  • Dismiss items you don't need this trip

Exporting Your List

Tap Export from the Shopping List screen to get a store-grouped, printable version of your list — useful for planning a multi-store shopping trip.

Price Tracking & Analytics

Viewing Price History for an Item

Tap any item in your receipt history or shopping list to see its full price history across all stores. You'll see the lowest price ever recorded and where, the average price, the highest price, and a side-by-side store comparison.

Analytics Dashboard

Go to Analytics to see your overall grocery spending:

  • Weekly spend — this week vs last week
  • Monthly spend — this month vs last month
  • Top stores — where you spend the most
  • Top items — what you buy most often
  • Spending trend — whether you're spending more or less over time
Pro tip: Scan receipts consistently for at least 4–6 weeks to build up enough price history for meaningful comparisons.

Troubleshooting

Receipt scan returns wrong items

Try a clearer photo with better lighting. Make sure the full receipt is in frame with no shadows. For very long receipts, try cropping and scanning in sections. For PDF receipts, make sure the PDF is a digital receipt (not a scanned paper receipt saved as PDF).

Items have generic names after scanning

Some stores use internal product codes on receipts. After saving, tap the item and rename it to something you'll recognize. The app will remember your correction.

App won't load or shows a blank screen

Clear your browser cache and reload the page. If the problem persists, try opening the app in a private/incognito window. You can also try adding the app to your home screen for a faster, more reliable experience.

Subscription not activating after payment

Your subscription status updates automatically after payment — this usually takes under 30 seconds. If it hasn't updated after a few minutes, sign out and back in. If the problem persists, contact support with your payment confirmation.

PDF upload fails

PDFs must be under 15MB and no more than 4 pages. Make sure your file is a real PDF (not a renamed image file). If your receipt PDF has many pages, try splitting it into smaller files.
